Research Programme

Two thrusts, one philosophy: materials that do work.

The group operates at the convergence of solid-state photochemistry and inorganic materials design — pursuing both fundamental questions and application-driven outcomes for actuation and energy storage.

01 / Photomechanical Crystals

Photo-chemistry & Molecular Actuators

Solid-state organic and metal-organic systems engineered around cycloaddition reactions ([2+2], [4+4]) to convert photon energy into mechanical motion via the photosalient effect — toward light-driven micromachines and adaptive materials.

Photosalient Effect [2+2] Cycloaddition Single-Crystal Motion
02 / Energy Materials

Battery Materials & Degradation

Metal-ion battery chemistries — lithium-ion, sodium-ion and emerging variants — with a structural focus on understanding the degradation pathways that bound lifetime, performance, and safety in real-world deployment.

Anode Materials Li-ion / Na-ion Lifetime Studies
